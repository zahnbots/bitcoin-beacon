A Senegalese Startup Wires Bitcoin Into Mobile Money

DAKAR, Senegal — In the markets of Senegal's capital, the money that clears fastest now rides two rails at once: mobile money on top, bitcoin underneath. Banxaas, built by the startup Yité Technologies, lets people swap between the CFA franc and bitcoin directly inside the mobile-money wallets they already use — Wave, Orange Money, MTN and others — and now reaches four countries: Senegal, Côte d'Ivoire, Guinea and Cameroon.

The pitch is not speculation. It is settlement. A trader in Dakar can be paid by a supplier in Abidjan, or a relative abroad, and receive francs on a phone in seconds — with bitcoin's Lightning Network doing the cross-border leg the banks either overcharge for or refuse. In June, a tool built on Banxaas won first place at the bitcoin++ hackathon in Nairobi for stitching West African mobile money to Kenya's M-Pesa.

Why it matters: francophone West Africa just got a bitcoin rail wired into the apps people already trust — no bank, no branch, no wait.
