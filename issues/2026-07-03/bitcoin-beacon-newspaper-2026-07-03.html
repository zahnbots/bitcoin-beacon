From Losers as Metaplanet Hits No. 3

Corporate accumulation grinds on — new European and SPAC entrants pile in — even as the speculative “premium era” collapses and weaker names trade below the value of their coins

Tokyo’s Metaplanet (3350) added another $170M in bitcoin this week, lifting its stack to 43,000 BTC and cementing it as the world’s third-largest corporate holder — behind only Strategy and Twenty One Capital (XXI). Notably, its bitcoin-options desk booked roughly $11M in Q2 operating revenue, a model that subsidizes accumulation rather than relying purely on share issuance.

But the fresh buying lands in a brutally reordered market. Some 198 public companies now hold a combined 1.268M BTC (~$77.5B) — yet the easy-money “premium era” that let treasury firms trade far above the value of their coins is over. Nakamoto, the self-styled “treasury for treasuries,” trades at a ~63% discount; a growing cohort of smaller entrants is exiting entirely, selling coins to repay debt or pivoting toward AI infrastructure.

Even the pioneer is adapting. Michael Saylor’s Strategy (847,363 BTC, avg. ~$66,385) rolled out a Digital Credit Capital Framework that formally breaks from its old “never sell” dogma — adding a USD reserve policy (~$3.8B liquidity coverage), up to $1B in MSTR buybacks, and selective bitcoin monetization.

New entrants keep coming anyway. Sweden’s H100 Group won shareholder approval to absorb two Norwegian treasuries, tripling holdings to ~3,500 BTC and taking Europe’s No. 2 spot behind Germany’s Bitcoin Group SE. Adam Back’s Bitcoin Standard Treasury (BSTR) debuts via SPAC with 30,021 BTC and a path toward ~53,500.

Banks & Custody

CITI. Citigroup confirmed plans to launch institutional bitcoin custody later this year, folding BTC into the same custody, reporting and tax rails it uses for securities and cash — and enabling cross-margining between digital and traditional assets.

MORGAN STANLEY. The $8T manager is rolling out spot crypto trading, has filed for spot BTC, ETH and SOL ETFs, and plans a proprietary digital wallet later in 2026.

STABLECOIN BLOC. Santander, BofA, Barclays, BNP Paribas, Citi, Deutsche Bank, Goldman Sachs, MUFG, TD and UBS are jointly exploring a 1:1 reserve-backed digital money — a rare show of collective big-bank interest in on-chain settlement.

FUNDS. BlackRock’s new covered-call iShares Bitcoin Premium Income ETF (BITA) is live; JPMorgan filed IBIT-linked structured notes (due 2028–2029) and now lets clients pledge spot BTC ETF shares as loan collateral. On flows, ETFs took in $222M Thursday — the best day in two months, ending a ten-day outflow streak — though YTD net remains −$5.4B.

MSTR. Benchmark’s Mark Palmer reiterated a Street-high $570 price target on Strategy, arguing the market is mispricing its bitcoin-per-share accretion even as treasury-stock premiums compress industry-wide.

CITI, CAUTIOUS. Citi cut its 12-month bitcoin target to $82K from $112K, citing June’s record ETF outflows, soft investor demand and slow crypto-legislation progress — while 10x Research’s Markus Thielen warned the cycle low could still be $46K–$47K.

Sovereign & State Reserves

FEDERAL. The Trump administration is finalizing the Strategic Bitcoin Reserve blueprint — Executive Director Bo Hines says the design is circulating among inter-agency groups and is slated for release before July 22. In parallel, the ARMA bill (Begich–Golden) would authorize up to 200,000 BTC/yr for five years on a 20-year hold, with quarterly Proof-of-Reserve audits, and would bar Washington from impairing Americans’ right to self-custody. The U.S. already holds ~328,000 BTC.

TEXAS. The Comptroller named a Strategic Bitcoin Reserve Advisory Committee and issued an RFP for custody and liquidity services — the state moving from statute to actual implementation.

WORLD. El Salvador holds ~7,500 BTC (purchases paused under its IMF deal); Bhutan has sold ~$1B in mined BTC over 18 months; Pakistan’s announced reserve still has no funded balance sheet, per its own central bank — a reminder to separate press releases from on-chain reality.

Self-Custody & Collaborative Custody

FOUNDATION. The Passport maker raised $6.4M (led by Fulgur Ventures; ~$16.5M total) and moved Passport Prime to general availability — pushing beyond a bitcoin wallet into a trademarked “Human Authority Hardware” category spanning identity, MFA and AI-agent authorization, all American-manufactured.

UNCHAINED × BITBOX. BitBox hardware wallets are now supported inside Unchained’s collaborative multisig vaults — widening key-device choice for its 2-of-3 model, concierge service, bitcoin-backed loans and IRA.

Governments & Regulation

GENIUS ACT. U.S. regulators must issue final stablecoin rules by July 18 — licensing, capital, custody and AML standards.

CALIFORNIA. AB 1052 took effect July 1, letting state and local agencies accept crypto as payment.

SOVEREIGNS. A new Bitcoin Policy Institute report counts 27 countries with BTC exposure, with 13 more advancing legislation.

Inside the Community

MINING. SBI Crypto will permanently shut its public pool on July 31, forcing ~20.9 EH/s (2.2% of hashrate) to relocate amid scrutiny over a flagged ~$21M in suspicious 2025 outflows.

DIFFICULTY. Fell ~9% in mid-June as listed miners redirect capacity toward AI/HPC; hashrate has recovered to ~1 ZH/s.

CONCENTRATION. Foundry USA (~24%), AntPool (~19%) and F2Pool (~14%) — the top five pools now direct more than 75% of block production.
