South Africa Put Bitcoin at 650,000 Checkouts

No mandate, no ministry — payment processors wired bitcoin into the QR code South Africans already scan at the till.

CAPE TOWN — The biggest bitcoin checkout network on earth wasn’t decreed; it shipped as a software update. MoneyBadger, a Stellenbosch payments firm, wired bitcoin into Scan to Pay — the QR code already taped to more than 650,000 South African tills. The customer scans the same code as everyone else in line; Lightning settles in seconds; the merchant receives rand and never touches a coin.

Pick n Pay proved the plumbing first, taking bitcoin at 1,500-plus supermarkets since 2023. Then the processors stacked up — Zapper’s 31,000 locations, Ozow, Peach Payments, Ecentric — until spending bitcoin in South Africa stopped requiring a map. Down the coast in Mossel Bay, Bitcoin Ekasi pays township surf coaches their whole salary in it.

Why it matters: adoption stops being a destination you visit and becomes a default you inherit — bitcoin riding rails people already trust.
