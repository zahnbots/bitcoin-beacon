39 Million Indians Own Bitcoin. Their Central Bank Wants It Banned.

A reserve-pilot push reaches Parliament this week — but the Reserve Bank of India is still arguing for prohibition.

NEW DELHI — Nearly 39 million Indians hold about $2.1 billion in bitcoin and other digital assets, and the country ranks first in the world for grassroots adoption. Its central bank’s advice to the government, revealed in documents reviewed by Reuters on July 8: lean toward prohibition.

The Reserve Bank of India warns that bank exposure to bitcoin invites contagion, that widespread adoption could accelerate capital flight, and that a country reliant on energy imports cannot afford money leaking past its banks. The tax department’s complaint is quieter: in one recent year, fewer than a quarter of Indians who traded crypto declared the gains.

The timing is pointed. A parliamentary committee hears the finance ministry around July 15 before reporting on whether India follows the RBI’s isolation or builds a licensing regime — while a ruling-party voice pushes a small bitcoin reserve pilot.

Why it matters: the largest grassroots bitcoin economy on earth is being governed by a state that would rather it didn’t exist.

The Take

Congress Moves to Stop Taxing Tiny Bitcoin Payments

A quiet reason Americans don’t spend bitcoin: every coffee bought with it is a taxable disposal. A House Ways and Means bill, H.R. 9178, would exempt the small network fees that ride along with a transaction — and it has bipartisan backing, including ranking Democrat Richard Neal.

It is a narrow fix, not the $200 de minimis threshold advocates wanted. But it concedes the real point: the friction that keeps bitcoin an investment rather than a currency in America is written in the tax code, not the protocol.

Policy & Nation-States

ISLAMABAD. Pakistan spent a year building a bitcoin framework — a regulator, a Virtual Assets Act, plans for a state reserve and surplus-power mining. Then its most influential cleric, Mufti Taqi Usmani, ruled crypto trading impermissible, and said renaming it “token” or “stablecoin” changes nothing. The fatwa isn’t law, but in a country of 240 million Muslims it may be the harder veto. Full story →

Markets & Institutions

ABU DHABI. Bitcoin Suisse’s Middle East arm won a full Financial Services Permission from Abu Dhabi’s ADGM on July 7, clearing it to offer custody, trading and hedging to institutions across the UAE. The Swiss firm safeguards about $3.7 billion in digital assets. While the West tightens and South Asia stalls, the Gulf keeps opening the front door. Full story →

Money & Macro

BUENOS AIRES. President Javier Milei signed Order 475/2026, exempting registered exchanges from Argentina’s 1.2% “cheque tax” on money moving in and out of them — a levy that had singled out crypto since 2021. In Latin America’s most active market, the state just made it cheaper to move pesos into bitcoin. Full story →
