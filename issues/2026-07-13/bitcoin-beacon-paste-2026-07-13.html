39 Million Indians Own Bitcoin. Their Central Bank Wants It Banned.

NEW DELHI — Nearly 39 million Indians hold about $2.1 billion in bitcoin and other digital assets, and the country ranks first in the world for grassroots adoption. Its central bank’s advice to the government, revealed in documents reviewed by Reuters on July 8: lean toward prohibition.

The Reserve Bank of India warns that bank exposure to bitcoin invites contagion, that adoption could accelerate capital flight, and that an energy-importing country cannot afford money leaking past its banks. The timing is pointed: a parliamentary committee hears the finance ministry around July 15 before reporting on whether India follows the RBI’s isolation or builds a licensing regime — while a ruling-party voice pushes a small bitcoin reserve pilot.

Why it matters: the largest grassroots bitcoin economy on earth is being governed by a state that would rather it didn’t exist.

Argentina Ends a Tax on Buying Bitcoin

President Milei signed Order 475/2026, exempting registered exchanges from the 1.2% “cheque tax” on money moving in and out of them — a levy that had singled out crypto since 2021. In Latin America’s most active market, the state just made it cheaper to move pesos into bitcoin. Full story →
