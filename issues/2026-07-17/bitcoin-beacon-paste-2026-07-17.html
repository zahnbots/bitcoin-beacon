A New York Lawsuit Wants to Seize 3.8 Million Idle Bitcoin

NEW YORK — A pseudonymous plaintiff called “Noah Doe,” joined by two Wyoming shell companies, has asked a Manhattan court to declare 39,069 bitcoin wallets abandoned property — and to hand over the keys. The wallets hold roughly 3.8 million bitcoin, near $290 billion, some of it likely mined by Satoshi Nakamoto and untouched since bitcoin’s earliest years. The theory: under New York’s lost-property statute, coins that haven’t moved in five or six years are “abandoned,” and a finder can claim title. A judge has already paused a default judgment after the first named defendant argued the coins were never lost. The Bitcoin Policy Institute has intervened, warning that if dormancy equals abandonment, every long-term holder on earth must move coins to prove they still exist.

Why it matters: self-custody only works if leaving your coins alone still counts as owning them.
