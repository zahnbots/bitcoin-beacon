A New York Lawsuit Wants to Seize 3.8 Million Idle Bitcoin

Anonymous plaintiffs are asking a Manhattan court to rule that 39,000 long-dormant wallets — perhaps Satoshi’s own — are abandoned property they can claim.

NEW YORK — A pseudonymous plaintiff called “Noah Doe,” joined by two Wyoming shell companies, has asked a Manhattan court to declare 39,069 bitcoin wallets abandoned property — and to hand over the keys. The wallets hold roughly 3.8 million bitcoin, near $290 billion, some of it likely mined by Satoshi Nakamoto and untouched since bitcoin’s earliest years.

The theory rests on New York’s lost-property statute: coins that haven’t moved in five or six years are “abandoned,” and a finder can claim title. A judge has already paused a default judgment after the first named defendant argued the coins were never lost at all.

The Bitcoin Policy Institute has intervened. Its warning is plain: if dormancy equals abandonment, every long-term holder on earth suddenly has to move coins to prove they still exist.

Why it matters: self-custody only works if leaving your coins alone still counts as owning them.

The Take

The Grid Blocked AI. Bitcoin Miners Cash In.

American towns are voting down data centers — the Bitcoin Policy Institute counts some $24 billion in AI projects stalled by zoning fights and grid fear. Yet the power, land and cooling those projects need already exist, humming, at bitcoin mines.

Public miners are now fielding more than $70 billion in offers to host AI compute. The industry politicians spent years calling a waste of electricity holds the one thing the AI boom can’t permit into existence fast enough: a substation that already says yes.
