Russia Legalizes Bitcoin for Foreign Trade

A sanctioned superpower just wrote a bearer asset into its export plumbing — the neutrality bitcoiners promised, aimed at the sanctions wall.

MOSCOW — On July 21, Russia’s State Duma passed a bill no G20 government had put in statute before: it makes bitcoin legal to settle international trade. Bill 1194918-8 recognizes bitcoin as property and opens a permitted lane for exporters to invoice oil, metals and grain in it — while banning bitcoin for domestic payments and keeping the ruble as the only money at home.

The design betrays the ambition. This is not bitcoin for the people; it is bitcoin for the treasury. Cut off from SWIFT since 2022, Russia has hunted for ways to get paid that skip a Western correspondent bank, and a bearer asset settles peer-to-peer — no message, no permission, no intermediary to lean on. Only eight licensed venues may handle the trades; retail is capped near $3,800 a year.

Why it matters: when a sanctioned superpower reaches for bitcoin to move its oil and grain, it concedes the whole argument — money no one can switch off is useful precisely because no one can switch it off.

The Take

Left and Right Agree: Keep the State Out of Your Wallet

A national survey found rare bipartisan agreement: Americans across the spectrum don’t want the government watching their money. The right fears the state; the left fears creditors and platforms — same demand, opposite doors. Self-custodied bitcoin answers it: no intermediary to approve, freeze, or reverse a payment. The honest caveat: bitcoin’s base layer is pseudonymous, not private, and self-custody is a responsibility, not a default. The point isn’t guaranteed anonymity. It’s the option to opt out.

Markets & Institutions

LUGANO. BancaStato, the government-owned cantonal bank of Ticino, switched on bitcoin buying and custody inside its existing banking app on July 23 — via Sygnum and Avaloq, with coins held off the bank’s balance sheet. It joins ZKB and St. Galler Kantonalbank; Sygnum’s partner banks now reach over a third of Switzerland. Custodial, not self-custody — but a state bank making bitcoin routine. Full story →

NEW YORK. Jack Mallers quit as CEO of Twenty One Capital on July 20, and Tether’s three-way merger of Twenty One, Strike and Elektron Energy collapsed; the stock fell ~18% to about $4.37. Mallers wanted bitcoin businesses with cash flow alongside the stack; the board wanted a cleaner holding vehicle. The company still holds 43,514 BTC (~$2.9B). Full story →

Network & Mining

GLOBAL. Galaxy Digital launched a $5M Bitcoin Quantum Readiness Initiative to fund post-quantum cryptography — years ahead of any machine that could threaten bitcoin’s elliptic-curve keys. The math is fundable; the hard part is coordinating millions of holders to move coins to quantum-safe addresses. The nearer fight: BIP-110’s data-filter signaling begins in August. Full story →
