Paraguay Earns More From Half as Many Bitcoin Miners

The state utility will collect a record $350 million from miners this year — while it plans to hand their electricity to AI.

ASUNCIÓN — Paraguay’s state utility ANDE expects a record $350 million in bitcoin-mining tariffs this year — up from $100 million two years ago — while its licensed miners fell from 71 to 41. The survivors are giants: four firms reserve 730 MW of hydro power, more than one Itaipú turbine produces. Multi-million-dollar deposits and higher rates cleared out the small farms; the tax authority chases gains at 10%; the first mining criminal case is in court in Villarrica.

Behind it all sits a date. Every mining contract expires December 31, 2027 — “non-negotiable,” says ANDE, which wants the electricity back for the AI data centers President Peña is courting. New money arrives anyway: Korea’s Bitplanet switched on machines at Paraguayan sites this month.

Why it matters: mining’s edge is renting stranded power. Paraguay is the test of what happens when a richer tenant shows up.

The Take · Opinion

Morocco Licensed What It Couldn’t Ban

Morocco banned crypto in 2017; Moroccans used bitcoin anyway, all eight years. The new licensing law — AMMC licenses, central-bank supervision, a 15% capital gains tax — is not a change of heart. It is an admission that the choice was never bitcoin or no bitcoin; it was taxed bitcoin or invisible bitcoin.

Policy & Nation-States

MUSCAT. Oman made its national mining pool, OmanHash, mandatory for every licensed miner — about 3% of the entire network, routed through one government pipe built by the same firm that runs Kazakhstan’s. Not a ban; a ledger. Full story →

PARIS. Bull Bitcoin asked France’s top administrative court to annul the decree transposing DAC8 — the rule that auto-reports every crypto holder to the tax grid. Its sharpest exhibit: 41 crypto kidnappings in France this year. A holder database, it argues, is a target list. Full story →

Markets & Institutions

HAWTHORNE. SpaceX joined the Nasdaq-100 with 18,712 BTC on its balance sheet. JPMorgan pegs the forced passive inflows near $4.3 billion — index funds now buy a bitcoin treasury without holding a view on bitcoin. Full story →

Money & Macro

CONCORD. New Hampshire’s Executive Council rejected, 3–2, the first attempt to put bitcoin inside a $100 million municipal bond. One vote short, in a state of 1.4 million — a schedule, not a verdict. Full story →

On the Ground

AUSTIN. Bitcoin Depot’s 9,000 kiosks — the largest bitcoin ATM network anywhere — are being liquidated in a Texas courtroom. Texans lost $56.8 million to kiosk scams last year; Iowa found 98% of the machines’ transactions were fraud. The cash on-ramp died of its users’ enemies. Full story →
