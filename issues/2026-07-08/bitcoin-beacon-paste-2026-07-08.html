Nigeria's New Loan Collateral Is Bitcoin

LAGOS — In Africa's biggest crypto market, the new thing to do with bitcoin is to not sell it. Busha, a Lagos exchange, has lent naira against customers' bitcoin since January: pledge the coin, borrow up to half its value, keep the upside, pay about 2% a month. TechCabal reports the product is spreading across Nigeria and South Africa as startups discover what banks have always known — lending is a profit centre, and crypto-rich, cash-poor customers are a market nobody else serves.

Nigeria's new tax code sharpens the logic. Selling digital assets now triggers up to 25% tax on gains, and exchanges must report every trade. A loan is not a disposal: the borrower gets naira, keeps the position, books no gain. Underneath sits a market that moved roughly $92 billion on-chain in a year.

Why it matters: the day lenders accept an asset as security is the day it starts functioning as capital.
