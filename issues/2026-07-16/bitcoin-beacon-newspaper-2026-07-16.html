Zimbabwe Puts Its Bitcoin Economy on the Books

Years after banks were barred from touching crypto, the state now demands every bitcoin dealer register — and takes a 2% cut of the trade it once tried to outlaw.

HARARE — On Harare’s pavements the exchange rate that matters isn’t the central bank’s: dollars in, bitcoin or a dollar token out, settled before the customer crosses the street. For years the trade lived in a legal grey zone. This month it got a rulebook. Statutory Instrument 99 of 2026 — Zimbabwe’s first virtual-asset framework — makes anyone who buys, sells, moves or stores crypto register with the Reserve Bank’s financial-intelligence unit, binding since June 16. The price of entry: US$500 a year, a 2% transaction levy, a 15% withholding tax on cross-border digital services. It is a sharp reversal for a state that in 2018 ordered banks to shun crypto — and never stopped the peer-to-peer trade that followed. Zimbabweans have buried two currencies in two decades; bitcoin and dollar stablecoins are how they keep this week’s earnings until next week.

Why it matters: when a government stops banning a money and starts taxing it, the argument is over.

The Take · Opinion

Taiwan Wants a Reserve China Can’t Freeze

A legislator’s push to put ~0.5% of Taiwan’s $602bn reserves — about $2.5bn — into bitcoin isn’t a bet on price. It is insurance: after 2022 the world watched half of Russia’s reserves frozen overnight. A self-custodied slice no foreign capital can switch off is a fire exit, not a yield play.

Money & Macro

MOSCOW. A Russian law in force since July 1 lets exporters settle oil, metal and grain in bitcoin and other digital assets; a two-year pilot reportedly moved ~$11bn. The ruble stays the only domestic money — but for getting paid across a sanctioned border, Moscow now reaches for the one asset no foreign bank can freeze. Full story →

Network & Mining

CIUDAD DEL ESTE. Paraguay’s utility has roughly doubled the mining tariff toward $0.06/kWh and warns half of operations could turn unprofitable. Licensed miners have fallen from 71 to 41 even as ANDE’s mining revenue triples to ~$350m — and every crypto contract expires end-2027, with AI data centres circling the same cheap power. Markets & Institutions

TOKYO. A Japanese upper-house committee backed reclassifying crypto as securities under the FIEA and capping the tax at a flat 20%, down from ~55%. The change clears the runway for spot bitcoin ETFs on the Tokyo Stock Exchange by 2027–28 — the same week South Korea moved the same way. Full story →

BISHKEK. Draft amendments would let Kyrgyzstan’s government mine bitcoin and build a “state cryptocurrency reserve” from mining, tokenised assets and a som-backed stablecoin — a river-rich, indebted republic betting it can manufacture a reserve from hydropower, with A7A5-stablecoin baggage attached.
