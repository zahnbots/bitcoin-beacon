South Korea Moves to Treat Bitcoin as a National Asset

SEOUL — At a July 15 briefing at the presidential office, South Korea's finance ministry said it will write virtual assets into the way the state itself is run. A planned National Asset Basic Act would replace a 1950s property law and, for the first time, list crypto alongside intellectual property as a category of national asset the government must formally manage.

In the same breath, officials said they will amend the Capital Markets Act to allow spot digital-asset ETFs — bitcoin products first in line — and advance a wider Digital Asset Basic Act in the second half of the year. Korean corporations were already cleared in January to hold bitcoin, capped at 5% of equity a year.

Why it matters: one of the world's largest economies is deciding bitcoin belongs inside the machinery of public finance, not outside it.
