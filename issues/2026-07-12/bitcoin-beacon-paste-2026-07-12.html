Bolivia Ran Out of Dollars. Bitcoin Filled the Gap.

LA PAZ, Bolivia — When the boliviano's dollar peg cracked and the banks ran dry, Bolivians opened wallets instead of waiting for a rescue. Virtual-asset transactions ran about $294 million in the first half of 2025, up from $46.5 million a year earlier — a jump of roughly 532%.

Most of that flows through dollar-pegged stablecoins. But the bitcoin layer is where the everyday spending lives: a La Paz coffee stand takes payment over a Blink Lightning QR, barbershops quote in satoshis, and a national QR standard (OpenBCB) opened to crypto wallets via AEON on July 9. Even the state energy firm, YPFB, has used crypto to pay for fuel imports it couldn't source dollars for.

Why it matters: when the dollars vanish, bitcoin is the rail with no correspondent bank left to say no.
