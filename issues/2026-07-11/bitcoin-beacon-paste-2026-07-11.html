Kazakhstan Cuts Taxes to Pull Bitcoin Onshore

ASTANA — The country that chased out its bitcoin miners in the 2021 crackdown now wants them back. On July 8 President Kassym-Jomart Tokayev signed a decree that exempts income from digital-asset trades on licensed Kazakh platforms from personal income tax, lets miners burn otherwise-flared gas for power, and builds on a state crypto reserve the National Bank valued last autumn at $500 million to $1 billion.

The logic is jurisdictional. Kazakhstan was briefly the world's #2 mining hub before blackouts and a ban scattered the rigs. The new rules try to pull that hashrate — and the traders who followed it offshore — back inside a taxable, licensed border.

Why it matters: a government that once treated bitcoin as a drain on its grid now treats it as an export.
