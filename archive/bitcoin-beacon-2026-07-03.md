# The Bitcoin Beacon — Friday, July 3, 2026

*Your daily read on Bitcoin and the macro forces moving it.*

## The Lead: Green July gets off to a solid start

Bitcoin is trading around **$61,800** this morning, up roughly 2.5% on the day, after a soft June jobs report reset rate-cut expectations in Bitcoin's favor. The economy added just 57,000 jobs and unemployment ticked to 4.2% — weak enough to cool fears of another Fed hike and push risk assets higher. Ethereum jumped more than 5% alongside it.

The bigger picture is still humbling. BTC is down roughly 50% from its $126,000 peak and has spent June grinding in a $57K–$60K band — its worst month in four years. Bulls are leaning on a seasonal pattern: every "red" June in recent memory has been followed by a "green" July. Traders remain split on whether $57K–$60K is the bear-market bottom or a way station toward $40K–$55K.

## Institutional & ETF Flows

The standout data point: U.S. spot Bitcoin ETFs pulled in **$221.7 million** on Thursday — their biggest single-day intake in two months — snapping a brutal 10-day outflow streak that had bled $2.73 billion. Fidelity's FBTC led with ~$166M in fresh money, while BlackRock's IBIT actually shed $40.4M on the day.

Don't celebrate too hard yet. Year-to-date net outflows still sit around **$5.4 billion**, and analysts are clear that one good day isn't a trend. Sustained inflows are the signal that historically confirms a durable recovery, and June was the worst month on record for ETF flows. Corporate treasury accumulation, notably, kept buying through the ETF weakness.

## Banks & Investment Firms

**BlackRock** launched the **iShares Bitcoin Premium Income ETF (BITA)** in June — a covered-call product writing options against part of its IBIT holdings for yield. CIO Rick Rieder reiterated he expects BTC "considerably higher" long term while keeping position sizes moderate.

**JPMorgan** continues its quiet pivot despite Jamie Dimon's long-running skepticism. The bank filed for structured notes tied to IBIT (auto-callable barrier notes due 2028–2029), its asset-management arm boosted its IBIT stake ~64% to $343M earlier this year, and it's expanded Bitcoin-backed lending — letting clients pledge spot BTC ETF shares as loan collateral.

## Governments & Regulation

- **GENIUS Act deadline looms:** U.S. federal and state regulators must issue final stablecoin implementation rules by **July 18** — covering issuer licensing, capital, custody, and AML standards. A key marker for how the post-2025 regulatory framework actually functions.
- **California AB 1052** took effect July 1, letting state and local agencies accept crypto as payment.
- **Nation-state adoption:** A new Bitcoin Policy Institute report counts **27 countries** with direct or indirect BTC exposure, with 13 more advancing legislation — continued momentum for Bitcoin as a strategic sovereign asset.

## Inside the Bitcoin Community

- **SBI Crypto** is permanently shutting its public mining pool on **July 31**, forcing ~20.9 EH/s (about 2.2% of global hashrate) to relocate. The Japanese pool has been under a cloud since ZachXBT flagged ~$21M in suspicious 2025 outflows.
- **Mining difficulty** fell ~9% in mid-June as several listed miners redirect capacity toward AI/HPC; network hashrate has since recovered to roughly 1 ZH/s.
- **Pool concentration watch:** Foundry USA (~24.3%), AntPool (~19.4%), and F2Pool (~14%) — the top five pools now direct well over three-quarters of all block production, a recurring decentralization concern.
