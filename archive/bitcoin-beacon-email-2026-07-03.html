The Lead

Green July gets off to a solid start

Bitcoin is trading around $61,800 this morning, up roughly 2.5%, after a soft June jobs report reset rate-cut expectations in Bitcoin's favor. The economy added just 57,000 jobs and unemployment ticked to 4.2% — weak enough to cool fears of another Fed hike and lift risk assets. Ethereum jumped more than 5% alongside it.

The bigger picture is still humbling. BTC is down ~50% from its $126,000 peak and spent June grinding in a $57K–$60K band — its worst month in four years. Bulls lean on a seasonal pattern: every “red” June lately has been followed by a “green” July. Traders remain split on whether this is the bottom or a stop on the way to $40K–$55K.
 

Institutional & ETF Flows

U.S. spot Bitcoin ETFs pulled in $221.7M Thursday — their biggest daily intake in two months — snapping a 10-day outflow streak that bled $2.73B. Fidelity's FBTC led with ~$166M; BlackRock's IBIT actually shed $40.4M on the day.

Temper the enthusiasm: year-to-date net outflows still sit near $5.4B, and one green day isn't a trend. Sustained inflows are the signal that historically confirms a durable recovery. Notably, corporate treasuries kept buying straight through the ETF weakness.
 

Banks & Investment Firms

BlackRock launched the iShares Bitcoin Premium Income ETF (BITA) in June — a covered-call product writing options against part of its IBIT holdings for yield. CIO Rick Rieder still sees BTC “considerably higher” long term.

JPMorgan continues its quiet pivot despite Jamie Dimon's skepticism: structured notes tied to IBIT (due 2028–2029), a ~64% boost to its IBIT stake ($343M), and expanded lending that lets clients pledge spot BTC ETF shares as collateral.
 

Corporate Treasuries — Strategy (MSTR)

Michael Saylor's Strategy holds 847,363 BTC (avg. ~$66,385). The headline shift: a new Digital Credit Capital Framework that formally breaks from the old “never sell” dogma — a USD reserve policy (~$3.8B liquidity coverage), up to $1B in MSTR buybacks, and selective BTC monetization. Shares also popped ~10% on an unrelated government defense-tech contract.
 

Governments & Regulation

• GENIUS Act deadline: U.S. regulators must issue final stablecoin rules by July 18 — licensing, capital, custody, AML.

• California AB 1052 took effect July 1, letting state and local agencies accept crypto as payment.

• Nation-state adoption: a new Bitcoin Policy Institute report counts 27 countries with BTC exposure, 13 more advancing legislation.
 

Inside the Bitcoin Community

• SBI Crypto permanently shuts its public mining pool on July 31, forcing ~20.9 EH/s (2.2% of hashrate) to relocate — the pool has been under scrutiny since a flagged ~$21M in suspicious 2025 outflows.

• Difficulty fell ~9% in mid-June as listed miners redirect capacity toward AI/HPC; hashrate has recovered to ~1 ZH/s.

• Pool concentration: Foundry USA (~24%), AntPool (~19%), F2Pool (~14%) — the top five now direct 75%+ of block production.
 

What I'm Watching

Whether Thursday's ETF inflow becomes a trend, the July 18 GENIUS Act deadline, and whether BTC can hold above $60K to validate the “green July” thesis.
